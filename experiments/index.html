This page indexes the project's digital-humanities experiments: bounded, internal calibration studies of how the frozen retrieval instrument behaves. Each experiment is a method study, not a results dashboard.

Everything here is metadata-only and describes experimental workflow. Detailed outputs — ranked lists, per-pair material, scores, and model outputs — are internal working records and are not published on this public site by design. Nothing here states or implies reading, source use, borrowing, contact, dependence, recall, validation, coverage, discovery, or influence. “Routing,” “surfacing,” “calibration,” “known-terrain,” and “candidate comparison” are used only as method terms.

Status labels are descriptive notes, not formal categories: completed internal calibration — the experiment was run internally and concluded; internal PASS — the instrument met its pre-registered routing/surfacing calibration criterion on known terrain (a channel-behavior result, not a historical finding).
