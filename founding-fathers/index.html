Founding Fathers

A comparison and review track for founding-era writings and Scottish Enlightenment materials.

The Founding Fathers track pairs founding-era writings with Scottish Enlightenment comparison materials and surfaces candidate textual intersections for scholarly review. A bounded internal calibration pilot, Founders-1C, has been completed and is summarized on the Experiments page. This page is the entry point to the track's catalogues and that pilot.

Note. No public pair-level finding is displayed here. Founders-1C is an internal calibration result — a check of how the retrieval instrument routes, not a published comparison of any two texts.

What the track compares

Two working catalogues. Founding-era writings — the collected papers of the founders (John Adams, Benjamin Franklin, Alexander Hamilton, John Jay, Thomas Jefferson, James Madison, and George Washington) and their contemporaries, with Founders Online as the principal public access point. Scottish Enlightenment materials — openly available scholarly and public-domain editions of David Hume, Adam Smith, Francis Hutcheson, Henry Home (Lord Kames), Adam Ferguson, John Millar, Thomas Reid, William Robertson, and others.

The final roster and date windows are fixed at corpus assembly. Browse the two catalogues via the cards below.

The Founders-1C calibration pilot

A bounded internal calibration pilot has been completed. It tested how the project's frozen retrieval instrument routes founder-essay queries to Scottish comparison neighbors on known essay-register terrain, and reached an internal PASS on routing calibration.

The pilot used public-domain, essay-register witnesses — The Federalist Papers, Hume's Political Discourses, Hume's Essays, and Kames's Sketches of the History of Man — not Founders Online full text. The method-level summary, corpus, and citable run identity are on the Founders-1C experiment page. No public pair-level finding is displayed.

Method

The completed Founders-1C pilot was a bounded, internal routing-calibration study: retrieval-level routing on known essay-register terrain, reviewed internally, and summarized as method-level metadata. It is a check of instrument behavior, not a published comparison.

Any further work — a larger routing pass, a structural-scoring stage, or a correspondence-scale study — is future, separately gated, and not run here.

Sources and permissions

The completed pilot's witnesses are public-domain transcriptions: The Federalist Papers and Hume's Political Discourses and Essays via Project Gutenberg, and Kames's Sketches via ECCO-TCP. Hume Texts Online was not the source for the completed public pilot.

Founders Online (National Archives and University of Virginia Press) remains the principal public access point for the founders' papers and a future correspondence-scale target, subject to sanctioned access and permissions review at the time of ingestion.

Online Library of Liberty and Hume Texts Online are additional scholarly-edition sources for possible future work, each subject to its own terms. All use will comply with licensing current at the time of ingestion.
