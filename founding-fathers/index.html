Corpus pairing

The track pairs two working catalogues and maps candidate textual intersections between them.

Founders' writings. The collected papers of John Adams and John Quincy Adams, Benjamin Franklin, Alexander Hamilton, John Jay, Thomas Jefferson, James Madison, and George Washington, together with correspondence to and from many contemporaries, drawing on Founders Online as the principal public access point.

Scottish thinkers. A working comparison catalogue assembled from openly available scholarly digital editions and public-domain transcriptions, subject to permissions review before ingestion. The provisional roster includes David Hume, Adam Smith, Francis Hutcheson, Henry Home (Lord Kames), Adam Ferguson, John Millar, Thomas Reid, William Robertson, George Turnbull, David Fordyce, and Dugald Stewart. Additional figures, including Hugh Blair, James Beattie, and Gershom Carmichael, may be considered where suitable digital editions can be verified. The final roster and date windows are fixed only at corpus assembly.

Method

The planned track applies the same layered methodology as the project's other tracks: mechanical passage-level retrieval over paired corpora, staged review, method records, and review tools. Its output would be a map of candidate intersections, including shared terms, parallel formulations, and review zones offered for scholarly inspection. Consistent with the project's scope note, the track does not claim influence, contact, source-dependence, discovery, validation, recall, or coverage.

Sources and permissions

Founders Online, from the National Archives and University of Virginia Press, is the principal public access point for founders' papers and related editorial material. Any project use of its data or editorial layer requires permissions review and compliance with current terms at the time of ingestion.

Online Library of Liberty provides public access to many relevant Scottish Enlightenment editions. Any project use requires permissions review and compliance with current Liberty Fund terms at the time of ingestion.

Hume Texts Online provides open scholarly editions of Hume. Terms must be confirmed with maintainers before ingestion.

ECCO-TCP and Project Gutenberg may be used for public-domain transcriptions, edition cross-checking, or roster extension where appropriate.

Source licensing is recorded as understood at project start; all use will comply with licensing current at the time of ingestion.
