About / Citation

Claim ceiling. This site presents approved source previews, catalogue materials, computational routing metadata, and review records to support scholarly inspection. It does not claim that Hume read, used, borrowed from, contacted, depended on, or was influenced by any listed text.

The Hume–La Flèche project builds a computational research instrument for disciplined candidate surfacing: reducing a very large space of possible text-to-text comparisons into ordered, provenance-clean review queues and compact candidate dossiers that qualified scholars can engage productively. The Historical Libraries Project is a scholarly research programme that studies early modern libraries and their texts as possible intellectual contexts for later philosophy. This public site is a generated research instrument: it presents catalogue records, approved source previews, and computational review tools for scholarly inspection. It does not publish findings of influence.

What this site is

Historical Libraries Project
A research programme building a computational instrument for disciplined candidate surfacing — reducing a very large space of possible text-to-text comparisons into ordered, provenance-clean queues that qualified scholars can review. It is a review-support programme; substantive historical interpretation belongs to human scholarly review.
La Flèche catalogue & app
A catalogue of the La Flèche / Riboulet library and the surrounding source materials, with per-text catalogue records, processing/coverage status, and, where cleared, readable source previews of the underlying texts.
British Contexts
A companion track that compares Hume's texts with English-language comparison texts (documented interlocutors, plausible comparators). It surfaces candidate comparison relations and provides review tools — the review signal map and the experimental retrieval matrix — for scholarly inspection.

How to read the review tools

  • The review signal map and retrieval matrix are scholarly review instruments, not a measure of historical relation. They show routing metadata — how the retrieval channels rank candidate pairs — to help a reader see what was surfaced for review and why.
  • The site surfaces candidate comparison relations for scholarly inspection. A surfaced pair is a routed candidate, not a conclusion.
  • Retrieval channels are shown separately and as rank/order; they are not combined into a single score.
  • The site establishes no claim of reading, source use, borrowing, contact, dependence, recall, validation, coverage, or influence. Establishing any such relation is a matter for qualified scholarly review, not for this instrument.

Review vocabulary — what the review labels mean

These are the review dimensions a reader encounters across the tools. Each is routing / review metadata that orders and describes what was surfaced for scholarly review — not evidence of a historical relation, and never a finding.

Review category (routing category)
Where a candidate sits by retrieval routing — for example a top-surface or near-surface band. It describes routing order, not evidential strength, and asserts no historical relation.
Magnet-suspect (routing flag)
A flag that a pair may have been over-promoted for mechanical reasons — a lexical or character-overlap route driven by cognates, OCR/apparatus artefacts, or formulaic overlap rather than shared argument. It marks a route to scrutinise, not a positive signal, and asserts no historical relation.
Review priority / tier (ordering prior)
An ordering prior that decides what a reviewer looks at first. It orders review attention only; it is not a measure of strength and asserts no historical relation.
Relation strength labels (provisional routing descriptor)
The labels distinctive, strong-moderate, and moderate are a provisional routing/triage descriptor of the retrieval signal for a candidate relation. They are not a measure of historical relation or evidential strength; they are legacy labels still under review, and they assert no historical relation.
Published observation / Review pending (display / gate status)
Whether a PI-reviewed observation is published for a dossier, or is still pending review. This is display and review-gate status only; it never stands in for a finding.
Passages available / not yet displayed (passage-display status)
Whether approved public excerpts are shown for a record, or are held out and not yet displayed. This is passage-display status only and asserts no historical relation.
Relation record · dossier preview · convergence zone (object types)
A relation record is one candidate pair's routing/review metadata; a dossier preview is a curated, passage-bearing review object; a convergence zone is a cluster grouping candidate comparisons for review. None of these is a finding — each is a review object, not evidence of a historical relation.
Catalogue entries vs distinct texts (count dimension)
The comparison catalogue lists text entries, and an entry can include a variant or duplicate edition of the same work — so a catalogue entry-count can be larger than the number of distinct texts that appear in candidate relations. Such counts describe the catalogue and its routing, not findings, and assert no historical relation.

How to cite

To cite this site, adapt the following site-level citation (replace the access date):

Historical Libraries Project, V2.1. Mark Collier, principal investigator. Public site generated from project data and served via GitHub Pages. Accessed [date].

When citing a specific track, page, or record, add its identifier — for example a British Contexts dossier id, a relation id, or a catalogue / source-preview record — and the page anchor shown on that page. Source previews are normalized working project texts (OCR-corrected, machine-assisted), not critical editions; cite the witness/provenance shown on the preview.

Acknowledgments

The English philosophical text layer draws on English Philosophical Texts Online (EPTO) files supplied by Amyas Merivale, whose structured text and paragraph-level metadata support citation, alignment, and review of the English comparison corpus. Public citation tokens are shown only where verified from that source metadata; where a token is not yet verified, a pending locator is shown instead.

Provenance & reproducibility

This public site is produced by a deterministic generator from the project's data and templates. Source previews are limited to an approved allowlist; other materials are held out. Technical build details (generator, template set, source-preview policy) are recorded in the build manifest that accompanies each build.
