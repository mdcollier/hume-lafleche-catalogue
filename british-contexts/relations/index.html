Relation records

British Contexts overviewCatalogueRelation recordsDossiersReview signal mapRetrieval matrix (experimental)

Relation records are compact catalogue entries for candidate comparisons in the British Contexts track. They organize Hume loci, comparison texts, review categories, passage-display status, dossier links, and convergence-zone membership where available. A relation record is not itself a dossier or an interpretive finding.

A relation record is a compact catalogue/index entry for one candidate comparison. A dossier is a curated, passage-bearing review object. A convergence zone is a problem-space / review-navigation cluster.

Relation records support scholarly navigation and review. They do not establish reading, source use, borrowing, contact, dependence, recall, validation, coverage, or influence.
