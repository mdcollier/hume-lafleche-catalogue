Method
Blinded review with a separated satellite / assembler division; reviewer-facing fields kept distinct from hidden consolidation fields.

What the review has produced so far

The current British Contexts review layer covers 39 candidate relations across 9 comparison texts by 7 British philosophers (Berkeley, Butler, Conway, Hobbes, Hutcheson, Locke, and Mandeville). The most recent full adjudication classified them into the review categories below. Eight bounded public dossier previews are currently available, covering 11 relation records; of their comparison-side citations, seven Amyas/EPTO tokens are verified and four remain pending.

9 Strong review candidates
a clear, specific parallel between the passages that is worth close scholarly review.
7 Plausible review candidates
a comparable parallel worth review, held at lower confidence.
1 Diagnostic method case
retained to show how the method behaves, not as a substantive candidate.
6 Likely magnet / artifact cases
the routing is likely driven by a lexical / orthographic artifact (a “magnet”), flagged for scrutiny rather than surfaced as a relation.
2 Insufficient public context
the public materials are not yet sufficient to review the comparison fairly.
14 Deferred pending source or passage review
held back pending source verification or passage authorization.

7 verified · 4 pending comparison-side Amyas/EPTO citation tokens. Verified comparison-side citation tokens are shown on their dossier pages; pending cases show a project locator until a token is verified from source metadata.

A few terms

Satellite reviewers
independent reviewers working separately from consolidation.
Assembler
the consolidation role that keeps reviewer-facing judgements distinct from hidden consolidation fields.
Parent-paragraph resolution
review at the level of paragraph-sized source units rather than whole works.
Locked evaluation rubric
the review categories were fixed before consolidation.
