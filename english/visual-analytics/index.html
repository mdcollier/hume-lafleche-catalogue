Experimental review visualization · for scholarly inspection. An experimental, in-progress surface built from the project's committed retrieval metadata. It shows routing / review structure — how the retrieval channels behave for each candidate relation — not a finding.

Review signal map Relation records

Integrated matrix

One cell per relation. Cell colour = public review category · red border ⚠ = magnet-suspect / methodological hazard · the three bars are the dense / IDF / Jaccard rank profile (three separate channels; taller = higher retrieval rank within that channel; routing order, not strength — never fused into one number) · the badge is the bounded rank (◆#) or the RRF rank (#), order only. Click a cell for full values; the cell also links to its relation record.

Reader primer — what the marks mean
Dense
embedding / model-based similarity channel.
IDF
distinctive-vocabulary overlap channel.
Jaccard
character 3-gram (surface / spelling) overlap channel.
Magnet ⚠
a review hazard where a passage may attract matches for mechanical reasons — lexical / formulaic overlap, cognates, OCR / apparatus effects, or other non-substantive features. A flag to scrutinise, not a positive signal.
◆#
bounded, public-ready rank / order.
#
RRF fused rank / order only (never a magnitude).
·
no resolved bounded pair for that relation — no scored channel values in this run's bounded pool (the relation still exists and still links to its record).

These are retrieval / routing channels. Channel agreement describes routing consistency, not evidential strength — and nothing here implies reading, source use, contact, dependence, or influence.

Advanced — raw per-channel panels (the seven-layer stacked view)

The seven layers below show the same grid once per channel, each on its own scale. The categorical routing layer is kept separate from the raw channels; no two layers share a colour scale; a single fused "strength" score is deliberately not produced.

Three worked examples

ENG-v02-023 · Hutcheson × THN 3.2.1
Lexical / Jaccard-heavy — dense-rejected (magnet-suspect)
dense #10698 · IDF #2009 · Jaccard #8 · RRF #44 · bounded ◆#44 · spread 10690
The character-overlap channel ranks it near the top while the dense channel rejects it — a likely orthographic/cognate coincidence. On the categorical map it reads as 'top-surface'; the channel split reveals it as a magnet to scrutinise, not to feature.
ENG-v02-019 · Hobbes × THN 2.3.9
Dense-driven — very high cross-channel spread
dense #1 · IDF #19 · Jaccard #137670 · RRF #1 · bounded ◆#1 · spread 137669
Dense ranks it near the top while Jaccard sits near pool-bottom — the opposite of a magnet. The disagreement is informative (dense-supported, surface-divergent) and is kept visible, not blended away.
ENG-v02-004 · Berkeley Dialogues × THN 1.4.5
All-channel agreement (contrast case)
dense #4 · IDF #11 · Jaccard #17 · RRF #1 · bounded ◆#1 · spread 13
Dense, IDF, and Jaccard all rank the pair closely (small spread) — the well-behaved case that makes the two atypical patterns above legible by contrast.
