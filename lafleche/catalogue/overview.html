Catalogue overview — coverage &amp; readiness

A whole-catalogue summary of source, digitization, and text-preparation readiness for the Riboulet holdings.

The Riboulet catalogue is a reconstructed record of the Collège de La Flèche library’s holdings, assembled as a bibliographical research dataset. This overview summarizes, at the whole-catalogue level, how far each stage of preparation has progressed — source-readiness, digitization-readiness, and text preparation — and what public access is recorded. The figures below are derived from the project’s catalogue indices and are separate from the per-record readiness chips on the catalogue page.

This is a historical holdings catalogue — a reconstruction of what the Collège de La Flèche / Riboulet library recorded. It is not a Hume reading list and not a comparison-results surface: no pair-level result, ranked list, score, or source passage is displayed here.

What this catalogue is

A reconstruction of the recorded holdings of the Collège de La Flèche / Riboulet library, presented as a bibliographical dataset for scholarly and collections-as-data research. For each catalogue record the project tracks, where known:

  • Source-readiness — whether a usable source witness has been located, verified, or cleared.
  • Digitization-readiness — whether a digital copy is located or acquired.
  • Text-preparation — whether a working text has been OCR’d and prepared.
  • Provenance & public access — the recorded source and, where available, a link to a public digital library. Provider links shown elsewhere on the catalogue are high-confidence public library records.

Detailed internal fields (eligibility, match confidences, pipeline state, and working records) are not part of the public catalogue by design.

Catalogue coverage

2,490 catalogue records are indexed. 2,089 have a digital source located; 401 do not yet. Language has been identified for 99 records so far: 51 French and 48 Latin.

Provenance & public-library links

1,737 records have a located record in a public digital library — 1,212 Gallica · 82 Google Books · 845 Internet Archive. A record may appear in more than one library, so these do not sum to the total.

Of these, 387 meet the high-confidence bar used for the catalogue’s public source-root links, counted by first qualifying library — 253 Gallica · 34 Google Books · 100 Internet Archive (each record counted once, in the fixed order Gallica, Google Books, Internet Archive).

A located record is not a verified one: only high-confidence matches are used as the catalogue’s public source-root links. These figures summarize recorded provider matches from the catalogue index; no live link-checking was run in this pass, and no per-record links, match details, or provider addresses are shown here. A digital source may be recorded (the 2,089 above) without a captured link in the three public libraries shown here: 352 records currently fall in that gap.
