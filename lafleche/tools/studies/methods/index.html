Experimental studies — method notes

Public-safe summaries of the experimental methods behind the project, translated from internal project documentation.

These notes describe methods and workflow only. They are summarized from internal project documentation and reports; the detailed records — ranked candidate queues, per-text metrics, scores, model outputs, and pair-level material — remain internal and, where noted, require PI review. Nothing here states or implies Humean influence, contact, source-dependence, reading history, discovery, validation, recall, or coverage. Method terms (retrieval, routing, embedding, similarity, comparison, analytics, validation, review) describe experimental workflow and scholarly-review support only. Frozen retrieval pipeline

Purpose
Surface candidate comparison material into ordered review queues.
Method
Sentence-bounded spans; a dense multilingual-embedding channel plus local lexical channels (IDF and character n-grams); reciprocal-rank fusion; parent-paragraph scoring under a fixed rubric. Frozen so results stay comparable across texts.
Substrate
Published philosophical and library texts in the project corpus.
Internal output
Per-text ranked candidate queues.
Method-level takeaway
The project's single frozen baseline, applied uniformly to every text; it supports review by ordering what a reader looks at first.
Why detailed results remain internal
Ranked queues are candidate-bearing.

Retrieval-channel ablation / evaluation replay

Purpose
Measure how much each retrieval channel and reranking variant contributes.
Source documentation
Internal Stage-1 retrieval evaluation replay report.
Method
Replay retrieval over a fixed evaluation set, ablating channels (dense only, IDF only, character-n-gram only) and rerank variants; no new retrieval run.
Internal output
Per-text, per-variant ranking metrics and ranked pairs.
Method-level takeaway
Channel contributions are language-dependent: the lexical channels rescue lexically-marked engagement in same-language comparison and add cognate/Latinism noise in cross-language comparison. All channels are retained so results stay comparable across texts.
Why detailed results remain internal
Per-text metrics and ranked pairs are result-bearing. Summarized from internal project documentation; detailed records remain internal pending PI review.

Section-aware / wide-net surfacing feasibility

Purpose
Assess surfacing material beyond a fixed top-K cut.
Source documentation
Internal section-aware feasibility study.
Method
A section-aware wide-net screening design as an additive extension — never a replacement of the frozen baseline — with audit-side accounting of what the fixed cut would miss.
Method-level takeaway
A fixed top-K cut is capacity-limited for texts with pervasive relations; the wide-net layer is an additive response and does not alter the frozen baseline. This is a surfacing/coverage-of-work question, not a claim about historical coverage.
Why detailed results remain internal
Surfaced material is candidate-bearing. Summarized from internal project documentation; detailed records remain internal pending PI review.

Alternative-embedding / competitor-model arc

Purpose
Evaluate alternative dense embedding models as candidate retrieval channels.
Source documentation
Internal competitor-embedding arc synthesis and auxiliary-channel memo.
Method
Compare challenger channels against the full frozen production stack on known terrain; blinded, cross-provider triage panels judge candidate zones. Comparisons are configured like-for-like.
Internal output
Ranked candidate queues, channel-overlap analyses, and blinded triage verdicts.
Method-level takeaway
No tested alternative dense model outperformed the full production stack on known terrain; one model was selectively adopted as a triggered auxiliary surfacing lens under specific conditions, never as evidence by itself. Known-terrain recovery is channel validation, not a finding about Hume's sources; retrieval disjointness between channels is not itself methodological value; challengers must be compared at full configuration against the full production stack.
Why detailed results remain internal
Candidate queues, overlap counts, and panel verdicts are candidate- and result-bearing. Summarized from internal project documentation; detailed records remain internal pending PI review.

Register-gap / cross-language similarity checks

Purpose
Measure the representational limits of paragraph-level similarity across languages and registers.
Source documentation
Internal cross-language bridge-test documentation.
Method
Doctrine-matched cross-language comparison tests over documented philosophical relations.
Method-level takeaway
Some documented relations are not visible at the paragraph-similarity layer — a representational limit of the method, not an OCR or contamination problem. This bounds what the similarity layer can and cannot surface.
Why detailed results remain internal
The specific similarity measurements are result-bearing. Summarized from internal project documentation; detailed records remain internal pending PI review.

Convergence / cap-casualty diagnostics

Purpose
Additive, read-only diagnostics over pre-cut convergence patterns.
Source documentation
Internal convergence-sidecar documentation.
Method
Distinctiveness-ranked sidecars (ranked by distinctiveness, never by raw volume) that annotate but never delete, demote, or reorder review queues.
Method-level takeaway
A feature layer that surfaces and annotates only; its precision at large scale is unvalidated, so its use is limited to review of already-tagged loci.
Why detailed results remain internal
Convergence loci are candidate-bearing. Summarized from internal project documentation; detailed records remain internal pending PI review.

OCR & normalized-project-text preparation

Purpose
Produce readable working source layers from page images.
Method
OCR followed by deterministic normalization — paragraph reflow, de-hyphenation, page-anchor preservation, and removal of markup and OCR-engine artifacts; no paraphrase, translation, omission, or invented text.
Method-level takeaway
The four public previews are the readable output layer; residual OCR or normalization errors may remain, as their caveats state.
Related public page
Source previews.

Source-faithful vs normalized text-layer taxonomy

Purpose
Define which text layers are appropriate for public display.
Method
A layered file / quality model distinguishing source-faithful, page-verified, and normalized reading layers, each with visible caveats.
Method-level takeaway
The public previews are the normalized reading layer and carry the normalized-working-text label — not diplomatic transcriptions or critical editions.

Cheap-tier attention / promoter studies

Purpose
Test whether inexpensive model-generated metadata can help order review queues.
Source documentation
Internal feature-role documentation.
Method
Cheap-model screening calibrated against panel and registry references.
Internal output
Per-pair attention metadata.
Method-level takeaway
Cheap-tier outputs are used only as an ordering / attention signal — never as a gate, a deletion rule, or a way to deprioritize protected material. An evaluation of one cheap tool as an autonomous gate failed and is on record; it is used as attention metadata only.
Why detailed results remain internal
Model outputs are result-bearing. Summarized from internal project documentation; detailed records remain internal pending PI review.

Direct-reading adjudication harness

Purpose
A disciplined close-reading adjudication step before anything is promoted for review.
Source documentation
Internal direct-reading adjudication protocol.
Method
A leak-scanned direct-read protocol with a hard pre-commit safety gate; source-bearing materials stay local-only and are never committed or published.
Method-level takeaway
Human / close-read adjudication is the mandatory layer before any pair or cluster is promoted; cheap-model or panel signals are calibration-only and never a gate.
Why detailed results remain internal
The reads are over source-bearing material. Summarized from internal project documentation; detailed records remain internal pending PI review.

English-comparator calibration arc

Purpose
Calibrate the instrument against documented English comparison texts.
Source documentation
Internal English-validation synthesis and split-design records.
Method
Development / validation / held-out splits under the frozen architecture (a development text with bounded refinement rounds, a validation text on the frozen architecture, and a negative control).
Internal output
Per-text scores and audit verdicts.
Method-level takeaway
Calibration evidence for evaluating the method — per-candidate calibration data, not a general rule and not a finding about Hume's sources; transfer to other substrates is unverified and carried as a future test.
Why detailed results remain internal
Per-text scores and audit verdicts are result-bearing. Summarized from internal project documentation; detailed records remain internal / require PI review.
