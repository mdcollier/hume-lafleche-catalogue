Experimental studies — method notes

Public-safe method documentation for the experimental work behind the project.
Claim ceiling. This site presents approved source previews, catalogue materials, computational routing metadata, and review records to support scholarly inspection. It does not claim that Hume read, used, borrowed from, contacted, depended on, or was influenced by any listed text.

These are public-safe summaries of the project's experimental methods and workflow, translated from its internal documentation. They describe the study question, the workflow, what was held fixed and what was varied, the kind of diagnostic used, the internal artifact produced, how the study shaped the public method or interface, and the method-level limitation it identified.

Raw and internal working outputs — ranked candidate queues, per-text metrics and scores, pair-level material, model outputs, and source-bearing reads — are not published on this public site by design; each note states which kind of material stays internal and why. Nothing here states or implies Humean influence, contact, source-dependence, reading history, discovery, validation, recall, or coverage. Method terms (retrieval, routing, embedding, similarity, comparison, analytics, validation, review) describe experimental workflow and scholarly-review support only. Frozen retrieval pipeline

Study question
How to reduce a very large candidate space into ordered review queues that stay comparable across texts.
Workflow
Build sentence-bounded spans → embed and score across channels → fuse the rankings → map surfaced spans back to parent paragraphs for rubric scoring → assemble a per-text review queue.
Held fixed
The whole pipeline is frozen, so outputs are directly comparable across every text and across method cycles.
Channels / layers
A dense multilingual-embedding channel plus two local lexical channels — IDF term weighting and character n-gram overlap — combined by reciprocal-rank fusion.
Internal artifact
Per-text ranked candidate queues.
How it shaped the public method
This baseline is what the catalogue's representation and processing counts summarize.
Method-level takeaway
A single frozen baseline supports comparability; it helps prioritize what a reader inspects first, and identifies material for review rather than establishing anything about it.
Internal outputs not published here
The ranked candidate queues are candidate-bearing and are not published on this public site.

Retrieval-channel ablation / evaluation replay

Study question
How much does each retrieval channel and each reranking variant actually contribute?
Workflow
Replay retrieval over a fixed evaluation set, turning channels on and off and swapping rerank rules — a re-analysis of already-committed runs, with no new retrieval executed.
Held fixed / varied
Fixed: the evaluation set and the frozen span construction. Varied: which channels are active (dense only, lexical only) and the rerank rule.
Diagnostic
Per-variant ranking comparison against the fixed evaluation set.
Internal artifact
Per-text, per-variant ranking metrics and ranked pairs.
How it shaped the public method
It is why all channels are kept rather than dropping the lexical ones.
Method-level takeaway
Channel value is language-dependent: the lexical channels rescue lexically-marked engagement in same-language comparison and add cognate/Latinism noise across languages. Keeping all channels preserves cross-text comparability.
Internal outputs not published here
Per-text metrics and ranked pairs are result-bearing and candidate-bearing, and are not published on this public site.

Section-aware / wide-net surfacing feasibility

Study question
Does a fixed top-K cut miss material for texts with pervasive relations, and can a wider net help without breaking comparability?
Workflow
Design a section-aware wide-net screening layer as an additive extension, and account on the audit side for what the fixed cut would leave out.
Held fixed / varied
Fixed: the frozen top-K baseline — the wide-net never replaces it. Varied: the breadth of the surfacing net at the screening layer.
Method-level takeaway
A fixed top-K cut is capacity-limited for pervasive-relation texts; the additive wide-net addresses this without altering the frozen baseline. This concerns how much has been screened (coverage of the work), not historical coverage.
Internal outputs not published here
Surfaced candidate material is candidate-bearing and is not published on this public site.

Alternative-embedding / competitor-model arc

Study question
Can an alternative dense embedding model improve candidate surfacing over the production stack?
Workflow
Run challenger channels on known terrain, compare against the full production stack (not a single channel), and send candidate zones to blinded, cross-provider triage panels.
Held fixed / varied
Fixed: the known-terrain texts and the production baseline. Varied: the embedding model and channel configuration.
Compared
Challenger dense channels against the full production stack (dense + lexical + fusion), configured like-for-like.
Diagnostic
Channel-overlap analysis plus blinded, cross-provider panel triage.
Method-level takeaway
No tested alternative dense model outperformed the full production stack on known terrain; one model was selectively adopted as a triggered auxiliary surfacing lens under specific conditions, never as evidence by itself. Two governing lessons: compare like-configured systems (full challenger vs full stack, not full-vs-partial), and retrieval disjointness between channels is not itself methodological value. Recovering known terrain is channel validation, not a finding about Hume's sources.
Internal outputs not published here
The candidate queues, channel-overlap analyses, and panel verdicts are candidate-bearing and result-bearing, and are not published on this public site.

Register-gap / cross-language similarity checks

Study question
What are the representational limits of paragraph-level similarity across languages and registers?
Workflow
Take documented philosophical relations and test whether the similarity layer surfaces them, matched by doctrine across languages.
Diagnostic
Doctrine-matched cross-language comparison over known relations.
Method-level takeaway
Some documented relations are not visible at the paragraph-similarity layer — a representational limit of the method, not an OCR or contamination problem. This bounds what the similarity layer can surface and is one reason human review remains essential.
Internal outputs not published here
The specific similarity measurements are result-bearing metrics and are not published on this public site.

Convergence diagnostics

Study question
Can pre-cut convergence patterns be surfaced for review without disturbing the frozen queues?
Workflow
Compute additive, read-only sidecars that annotate convergence, ranked by distinctiveness rather than by raw volume.
Held fixed
The capped review queue — the sidecar never deletes, demotes, or reorders it.
Diagnostic
Distinctiveness-ranked convergence annotation.
Method-level takeaway
A feature layer that surfaces and annotates only; its precision at large scale is a known method-level limitation, so its use is confined to review of already-tagged loci — never deletion or reordering.
Internal outputs not published here
The convergence loci are candidate-bearing and are not published on this public site.

OCR & normalized-project-text preparation

Study question
How to turn page images into readable working source layers safely and reproducibly.
Workflow
OCR the page images → deterministic normalization (paragraph reflow, de-hyphenation, page-anchor preservation, removal of markup and OCR-engine artifacts) → publish the normalized layer as a preview.
Held fixed
Normalization is meaning-preserving: no paraphrase, translation, omission, or invented text.
How it shaped the interface
The four public previews are this pipeline's output; their caveats state that residual OCR or normalization errors may remain.

Source-faithful vs normalized text-layer taxonomy

Study question
Which text layers are appropriate for public display?
Workflow
Define a layered file / quality model — source-faithful, page-verified, and normalized reading layers — each with a display policy and visible caveats.
Method-level takeaway
The public previews are the normalized reading layer and are labelled as such — not diplomatic transcriptions and not critical editions.

Cheap-tier attention / promoter studies

Study question
Can inexpensive model-generated metadata help order review queues cheaply?
Workflow
Run a cheap-model screen over pairs and calibrate its signal against panel and registry references.
Held fixed / varied
Fixed: the review universe — nothing is deleted. Varied: the cheap-model prompt and schema across iterations.
Internal artifact
Per-pair attention metadata.
Method-level takeaway
Cheap-tier outputs are used only as an ordering / attention signal — never as a gate, a deletion rule, or a way to deprioritize protected material. An evaluation of one cheap tool as an autonomous gate did not meet the bar, so it is used as attention metadata only.
Internal outputs not published here
The model outputs are result-bearing and are not published on this public site.

Direct-reading adjudication harness

Study question
How to adjudicate candidate pairs by close reading before any promotion, safely?
Workflow
A disciplined direct-read protocol with a hard, automated leak-scan pre-commit gate; source-bearing materials stay local-only and are never committed or published.
Method-level takeaway
Human / close-read adjudication is the mandatory layer before any pair or cluster is promoted; cheap-model or panel signals are calibration inputs, never a gate.
Internal outputs not published here
The reads are over source-bearing material; source-bearing reads are not published on this public site.

Null and negative controls are a standing part of the methodology; they help separate genuine signal from artifacts before anything is escalated.

English-comparator calibration arc

Study question
How does the instrument behave against documented English comparison texts?
Workflow
Development / validation / held-out splits under the frozen architecture — a development text with bounded refinement rounds, a validation text run on the frozen architecture, and a negative control.
Held fixed / varied
Fixed: the frozen architecture on the validation and held-out texts (no tuning). Varied: refinement only on the development text.
Internal artifact
Per-text scores and audit verdicts.
Method-level takeaway
Calibration evidence for evaluating the method — per-candidate calibration data, not a general rule and not a finding about Hume's sources. Whether it transfers to other substrates is an open method-level question carried as a future test.
Internal outputs not published here
The per-text scores and audit verdicts are result-bearing and are not published on this public site.
