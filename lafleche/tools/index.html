Experimental humanities tools

The project's experimental-infrastructure layer: methods, review workflows, and analytics that support scholarly inspection.

This area gathers the project's experimental humanities infrastructure — catalogue-oriented analytics, retrieval and routing orientation, comparison and review records, review-zone interfaces, and interpretive-review support. It complements the catalogue layer: where the catalogue is for browsing the Riboulet catalogue, source previews, and section structure, this layer is for the experimental methods and scholarly-review workflows behind it.

Everything here is metadata-only: it identifies material for scholarly review and does not display source texts or establish Humean influence, contact, source-dependence, reading history, discovery, validation, recall, or coverage. Surfaces marked in development describe intended future public-safe additions; they are not yet available and expose no findings, results, scores, model output, or source passages.

Section-level orientation over the Riboulet catalogue — represented vs not-yet-searched sections, entry and record counts, and the interactive coverage map. Lives in the catalogue path. Metadata-only; not an influence map and not a full-catalogue coverage claim.
Experimental studies & methods
A public-safe index of the experimental workflow behind the project — retrieval and ranking, embeddings and similarity, catalogue analytics, OCR and source preparation, review-zone and interface studies, and validation / null checks. Method-level summaries only; detailed results remain internal.
