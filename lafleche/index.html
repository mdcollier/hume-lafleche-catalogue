La Flèche / Riboulet Catalogue

The library environment of the Collège de La Flèche, where David Hume worked in the 1730s.

This section reconstructs part of the Riboulet catalogue of the Collège de La Flèche as a scholarly working environment. It has two complementary layers: readable source previews of selected texts, and a set of catalogue and comparison tools for orienting yourself in the wider library. Start with whichever fits your question.

Source previews
Read the working source texts prepared for the catalogue. Four are available now: Babin, Malebranche's Entretiens and Traité de l'amour de Dieu, and Burlamaqui.
Catalogue & comparison tools
Explore the Riboulet catalogue and its Hume-comparison layers — a coverage map, review zones, and the original scholar interface. Metadata-only; no source text.

Source previews are working project texts — OCR-corrected or normalized for readability, not critical editions; residual errors may remain. The comparison tools are metadata-only and identify material for scholarly inspection. The site makes no claim of Humean influence, contact, source-dependence, discovery, validation, recall, or coverage. Incomplete interfaces and remediation previews are kept in the archive.
